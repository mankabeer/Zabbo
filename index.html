The Card Game

Zabboo is a card game where a player must throw away their bigger card to be free. Whoever is left holding the heaviest cards — the biggest baggage — becomes the Donkey.

In life, as in the game, freedom comes from letting go.

The Narrative

Story

Apoorva & Anandi are a corporate couple based in Bangalore — 'happily married' with well-to-do families on both sides. Overtly they are a caring, intelligent, witty, smart, attractive couple. Yet beneath the surface, both are quietly unravelling in the stagnancy of their shared life.

The film begins with Anandi and Apoorva trapped in the same evening traffic jam in Bangalore — in different cars. Apoorva accidentally meets his ex-best friend Poorna, an unmarried, independent actress working in experimental theatre in Mumbai. After chatting with her, Apoorva remembers his past — and, for reasons he can't explain, is unable to share this meeting with Anandi. Anandi discovers the encounter through Poorna's Instagram post but chooses not to question his secrecy.

Both of them watch a couple game in a mall about knowing everything about each other. Apoorva's boss Gautam and his wife Devaki secretly admire them as the ideal couple — but watching Gautam and Devaki's drunken behaviour at dinner, Apoorva and Anandi grow all the more confused about their own happiness. Are we bored with each other?

Then comes the twist: Anandi, visiting Goa for a conference, accidentally meets Asif — a look-alike of Apoorva. Mesmerised by his personality, she spends a tender, dreamy weekend with him, convinced it is a surprise prank arranged by her husband. She wakes alone in a beach shack and returns home to find 'normal' Apoorva — completely unaware of any Goa surprise.

Complicating matters further, Anandi's ex-boyfriend Rohan — a classical singer from the USA — drops in and reawakens the dormant singer in her. Meanwhile, Asif appears again in Bangalore, confessing his feelings and urging Anandi to leave her boring husband. Neither Apoorva nor Anandi is ready to lose. Nobody admits defeat. The ego tussle escalates into a high-stakes blind game.

As the game reaches its climax — who wins at Zabboo? Who is ready to shed the heavy cards of ego to be free? Who becomes the Donkey? And what is the real meaning of being together? 'And they lived happily ever after' is a predictable, boring concept. Living in the moment, constantly inventing happiness — that is the key.

7 Episodes

Structure

EP. 1

Wrong Sequence

Apoorva and Anandi separately trapped in traffic. Apoorva meets actress Poorna after years. Anandi finds Poorna's Instagram post. A Sunday mall visit and a mediocre couple-game leaves them questioning themselves.

EP. 2

The Bluff

A bitter fight over the couple-game. A late-night video call from Anandi's sister about her husband's suspicious behaviour ignites new doubts. The card game Zabboo sparks further discord. Apoorva's boss invites them to Poorna's experimental play.

EP. 3

Poker

They watch Poorna's play. Anandi secretly compares herself with the vibrant actress. A dinner with the boss descends into drunken revelations. Anandi announces she will cut contact with Apoorva during her Goa conference.

EP. 4

Trump Card

Anandi in Goa, alone on her birthday. She follows a mysterious stranger — Asif — Apoorva's exact double. He takes her to a beautiful island. Certain it is her husband's prank, she surrenders to a dreamy evening. She wakes alone.

EP. 5

Shuffle

Anandi returns to a perfectly normal Apoorva. Rohan, her ex-boyfriend and classical singer, drops in from the USA, reawakening something dormant in her. Asif's wild photographs from Goa arrive. The confusion deepens.

EP. 6

Challenge

Asif reappears in Bangalore, confessing his feelings. He vanishes before Anandi can expose the prank. A family gathering in Goa. Both are judging each other. Apoorva is lured into a VR gaming room, where a masked woman calling herself Anna mocks his narcissism and slips a card into his pocket.

EP. 7

Showdown

Apoorva follows the card's address to a pub where a masked singer drags him by motorbike to a dark hilltop and declares Anandi is leaving him forever. He returns frantic — to find her perfectly ordinary at home. Asif and Anna meet as strangers on that same hill. Apoorva and Anandi's playful alter-egos finally merge — and a new chapter begins.

Tone & Vision

The World of Zabboo

Playful Existential Escape

Modern-day Marathi young urban couples are experiencing a subtle existential crisis that turns into meaningless couple quarrels. They will identify with protagonist characters who play an adventurous blind game — taking risk in their relationship, ultimately helping them playfully explore new horizons in friendship.

Cinematic Style

Zabboo is a slice-of-life comedy with surprising thriller elements. Urban claustrophobic chaos will be depicted in a crisp, speedy manner — juxtaposed with picturesque quietude and vibrant, free milieu in unexplored places of Goa.

"Playfully unpredictable, vividly colorful, sometimes flamboyantly bold and at times delicately subtle — a bouquet of enjoyable emotions."
